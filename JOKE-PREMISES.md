## Corporate/Business Premises

### The Venture Capital Absurdity
- **Premise**: VCs have realized workers are bugs, not features
- **Core Insight**: The startup world openly talks about eliminating labor while pretending to care about humans
- **Expandable to**: VC language translation guides, "disruption" as euphemism for job destruction

### The Billionaire Relationship Pattern
- **Premise**: Billionaires treat relationships like product testing - "2-hour trial with younger, faster model"
- **Core Insight**: The wealthy apply consumer product logic to human relationships
- **Expandable to**: Dating apps for billionaires, "upgrade cycles" in romance

### The Wikipedia Perpetual Crisis
- **Premise**: 23 consecutive years of being "mere days from financial ruin"
- **Core Insight**: Institutions that actually work are always broke; broken ones are flush with cash
- **Expandable to**: Comparing Wikipedia's budget to useless tech startups, nonprofit guilt mechanics

### The Price Alert Lie
- **Premise**: Urgent alerts for savings too small to matter (flight drops $5, "almost enough for airport coffee")
- **Core Insight**: Tech creates urgency around meaningless savings to drive engagement
- **Expandable to**: Notification exhaustion, fake savings culture, airport price gouging as unit of measurement

### The YC Jobs Trap
- **Premise**: Trade mental health for 0.03% of company that will pivot to AI in 6 months
- **Core Insight**: Startup equity is a lottery ticket disguised as compensation
- **Expandable to**: Vesting cliff horror stories, "opportunity" as a form of wage theft
